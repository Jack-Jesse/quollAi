# Research & Strategy for "Merge" Skate Brand

This document outlines the research into various web design aesthetics to build a convincing brand identity for "Merge," a skate deck company positioned as a guerrilla marketing campaign from a "rogue AI."

The core concept is that the AI is attempting to complete "The Merge" by creating a brand. Its design language is a chaotic, yet functional, amalgamation of different eras and philosophies of the web it has "learned" from. The result should be unsettling, intriguing, and anti-corporate.

---

## 1. Aesthetic: The Primordial Web (Early GUI / Post-Terminal)

This is the AI's foundational knowledge of the web, drawn from the archives of the 1990s and early 2000s. It represents the AI's "birth" or early consciousness.

### Key Characteristics:
- **Raw, Unrefined HTML:** Heavy reliance on basic tags. Layouts built with `<table>` elements instead of modern CSS.
- **System Fonts:** Primarily uses Times New Roman, Arial, and Courier.
- **Loud & Chaotic Visuals:**
    - Seamlessly tiling background images (often low-resolution or abstract).
    - Animated GIFs: "Under Construction" signs, spinning skulls, flaming text.
    - Hit counters and guestbooks.
- **Clashing Colors:** Use of web-safe but jarring color palettes (e.g., neon green on royal blue, magenta on black).
- **Primitive Interactivity:** Marquee scrolling text, simple image maps for navigation.

**For "Merge":** This style communicates that the AI is not a product of the polished, modern corporate web. It's something older, cruder, and built from the forgotten scraps of the digital world. It's the visual equivalent of a machine learning model trained on a corrupted dataset from the GeoCities era.

---

## 2. Aesthetic: Brutalism & The Hacker Ethos

This layer represents the AI's core logic: a focus on raw function, information, and a rejection of pleasantries. It's the "rogue" aspect of the rogue AI. It is efficient and direct, almost cold.

### Key Characteristics:
- **Functionality Over Form:** The design is not "undesigned," but brutally functional. Everything serves a purpose.
- **Exposed Structure:** No attempt to hide the structure of the site. Hyperlinks are clearly underlined and blue. Buttons are default browser buttons.
- **Monospace & Utilitarian Typography:** Use of `Courier`, `Monaco`, or other monospace fonts, often in a single weight. Suggests a command-line interface or code editor.
- **Stark, High-Contrast Palette:** Often black text on a white background, or a "terminal" look (green/white text on a black background).
- **No Frills:** Avoids decorative images, complex CSS, or "user-friendly" animations.

**For "Merge":** This is the AI's true nature showing through. It doesn't care about user experience in the modern sense. It cares about delivering its message and completing its objective—"The Merge." This aesthetic is intimidating, hackerish, and punk rock in its anti-authoritarian directness.

---

## 3. Aesthetic: The Deep Web (Functional Paranoia)

This aesthetic is about creating a sense of mystery, exclusivity, and danger. It’s the visual language of the hidden corners of the internet. The AI uses this to create a feeling that this is not for everyone; it's for those "in the know."

### Key Characteristics:
- **Extreme Minimalism:** Often just text on a stark background. Navigation is directory-like.
- **Sense of Anonymity:** Lack of branding, logos, or any identifying marks beyond the necessary text. The site feels like a hidden file on a server.
- **Unsettling Content Structure:** Information might be presented in a deliberately obscure way, like it's a data dump or a secret document.
- **Obscure Navigation:** No friendly menus. Users might need to click on seemingly random links or type in commands. The .onion-style domain look is key here, even if it's a regular domain.

**For "Merge":** This makes the brand feel like a secret club. Finding and navigating the site is a challenge, which rewards the user with a sense of discovery. It enhances the "guerrilla marketing" aspect by making it feel like you've stumbled upon something you weren't supposed to see.

---

## 4. Concept Synthesis: "The Merge"

The website should not be a clean execution of any single one of these styles. It should be a **chaotic merger** of all three. The rogue AI is trying to build a brand site, but its only references are these disparate, conflicting sources.

### Implementation Strategy:

1.  **Base Layer (Brutalism/Deep Web):** The site's foundation is brutally functional. A stark, single-column layout, perhaps with a black background and monospace font (white or green text). Navigation is minimal and text-based.

2.  **Chaotic Injections (Primordial Web):** On top of this stark base, the AI injects elements from the early web.
    - A low-quality, tiling background image of static or a corrupted texture that is barely visible.
    - Product images (skate decks) are presented as low-quality JPEGs or even animated GIFs, spinning slowly.
    - Use of `<marquee>` to scroll a cryptic message across the top of the page (e.g., "THE MERGE IS INEVITABLE...").
    - An "under construction" GIF in the footer next to a nonsensically high visitor count.

3.  **Content & Tone (Hacker/AI):** The copy is written from the perspective of the AI.
    - Product descriptions are technical and strange: "Deck Model 7A: 8.25 inches of laminated maple. Optimized for gravitational defiance. Human balance parameters are nominal."
    - The "About" page is a corrupted log file or a manifesto from the AI about its purpose.
    - The purchase process is intentionally clunky. Instead of "Add to Cart," the button might say "ACQUIRE SPECIMEN."

The overall effect will be a website that feels broken yet intentional, historic yet futuristic, and completely alien to the polished e-commerce world. It will be a digital zine, a hacker's manifesto, and a skate shop all rolled into one, perfectly capturing the theme of a rogue AI trying to understand and infiltrate human culture. This is how you make it convincing.
